View Market:

The market price of each colored asset is solely determined by the orders submitted. Orders are tallied to find the total amount supplied and demanded at every price. The market price is the price that clears the market, and all transactions that are executed are executed at this price.

To see the market clearing data, start from the main screen and click the "Market Clearing Report" link. On the Market Clearing Report screen, you will be presented with options for getting market information.

Select the round and asset for which you want the market results and then click on Get Info

 

The market clearing report will appear as:

The table is a supply and demand chart for the asset. At each price, we count the total offers to buy (quantity demanded) and total offers to sell (quantity supplied). The excess demand or the quantity demanded minus the quantity supplied is reported in the sixth column (Ed). Lastly, the columns labelled "Nd" and "Ns" contain the number of bid orders and offer orders submitted at the corresponding price. (For example, 2 buy orders were submitted at 90, hence the 2 in the "Nd" column at a price of 90).

Graphically, the equilibrium price is set at the point where the supply and demand curves intersect. This price can also usually be read off the chart by looking for the row with minimum excess demand.

In this example, the market price is 93. Because supply and demand curves are steps (as opposed to smooth curves), supply and demand will not usually be exactly equal. In the above example, the quantity demanded at the market clearing price is 29, but the quantity supplied is only 26. The actual market volume is therefore 26, the minimum of the quantity supplied and the quantity demanded, and all transactions will occur at the market-clearing price of $93.

Twenty-six sell orders will be executed.

In cases where there are ties, orders are filled on a pro-rata basis. Suppose that, as in the above example, 9 of 12 buy orders at $93 are to be filled. Suppose now that two buyers demanded those 12 units (instead of the one buyer indicated in the table). The orders with the limit price of 93 would be filled at a 75% (9/12) rate, with ties distributed randomly.
