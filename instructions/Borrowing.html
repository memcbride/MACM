Borrowing and Short Sales:

You may borrow money (at zero interest) to buy assets. This will occur automatically if you spend more than your available cash. The amount spent will be subtracted from your cash potentially resulting in a negative balance after the transaction.

Short selling is also allowed. That is, you may place orders to sell more of any asset than the amount you own. If you short sell one of the assets, you will have a negative asset balance shown on your report. When the asset is liquidated, you will receive the negative of the liquidation value. For example, if you hold -100 Blue shares and the liquidation value is 15, the $1500 will be deducted from your final net worth. If instead, the liquidation value was -30, then $3000 would be added to your net worth.
