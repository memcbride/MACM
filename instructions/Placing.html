Placing Orders:

All orders are limit orders. Suppose you wish to buy an asset. The limit order will contain the amount you want to buy and the maximum price per share you are willing to pay. Depending on market supply and demand, you may end up buying either at the price you bid, or at a lower price. You will never pay a higher amount. However, it is possible that your order may not be executed. Sell orders are similar except the sale price will never be less than the amount you enter for the order.

To place an order, look for the section entitled "Enter a Limit Order." The section is reproduced below.

Enter a Limit Order

Please fill in the following infomration.

  1. Select the Asset from the pull down menu.
  2. Select either Bid or Offer from the pull down menu.
  3. Enter the Quantity in the space provided.
  4. If entering an Offer, enter a minimum Price you are willing to sell at.
  5. If entering a Bid, enter the maximum Price you are willing to pay.
  6. Click on the "Submit Order" button when you have entered all the information.

You must place several orders if different bid prices (or sale prices) are desired. For example, you may be willing to buy ten BLUE assets at a price of $40 per share, and ten additional assets at a price of $30 per share. You must place two orders to buy BLUE completing each order as described above. If the market price turns out to be $25, you would buy twenty at $25 apiece, but if the market price turns out to be $35, then you would end up buying only ten BLUE at $35 apiece.
